# Vox Cambridge Reverb (V1031/V1032) — Electronics Rebuild

Complete electronics rebuild of a Vox Cambridge Reverb solid-state guitar amplifier:
the original electronics are gutted and replaced with modern parts while preserving
the original functionality, the front/rear panel layout (no new holes), and the
chassis. Guitar-frequency and 10″ alnico-speaker optimized throughout.

> **Honesty up front:** the Part 1–10 design docs were **reconstructed** from a prior
> conversation (see `docs/PROVENANCE.md`); some values were gap-filled. The KiCad
> project, BOM, and ngspice sims are **generated and tool-verified** (ERC/DRC/SPICE
> all pass), but the board is **placed, not routed**, and a few stages are designed
> substitutes. Treat the signal chain and power section as *validated design intent*,
> and re-verify part numbers/values before ordering or building.

## Core design decisions

| Block | Replacement |
|-------|-------------|
| Power amp | LM1875T (replaces germanium push-pull; eliminates driver transformer T2) |
| Preamp JFETs | MMBF5457 (SOT-23 on adapter) or J113 (2N5457 discontinued) — **availability watch** |
| Reverb / tremolo | TL072 ×2 (no transformer T3): IC1 = tank driver + **active wet/dry summer**; IC2 = tremolo LFO + **output buffer** |
| Reverb pan | Accutronics 4FB2A1C (~1475 Ω input) — 8 Ω 4AB3C1B is **incompatible** |
| Tremolo modulation | Xvive VTL5C1 or DIY LED/LDR optocoupler |
| Power | LM317T-regulated +17 V preamp rail (split `VBIAS_R`/`VBIAS_T` mid-rails); KBP410G bridge; `VREG_IN` RC pre-filter |
| Tone | passive Vox-style "cut" (designed substitute — original 25-5274-2 values not recovered) |

## What's verified (all via real tools, in CI-able commands)
- **Schematic:** generated by `kicad/gen/gen_kicad.py` → 8 hierarchical sheets, **ERC 0 violations** (KiCad 8). 117 components.
- **PCB:** generated by `kicad/gen/gen_pcb.py` → all footprints placed, GND pour, 190×115 mm outline. **Placed, not signal-routed** (KiCad has no headless autorouter). A `power_section_demo` board routes the rails **DRC-clean**.
- **BOM:** generated from the schematic by `kicad/gen/gen_bom.py` (1:1 with the netlist, no drift) → `bom/bom.csv` + `bom/bom-grouped.csv`.
- **SPICE:** `spice/run_all.sh` runs 7 ngspice blocks (preamp bias, reverb driver, MRB, tremolo LFO, power amp, **rail-aware** reverb summer, tone) and reproduces the documented numbers.

## Honest engineering notes (from the audit/roast passes — see `docs/roast-2026-06-16.md`)
- 🔴 **Output power ≈ 12 W, not 18 W.** A single 33.5 V rail can't swing the 17 V peak that 18 W into 8 Ω needs; the LM1875 ceilings at ~12 W clean (~1.8 dB down). Fine for a 10″ combo.
- 🔴 **Fit a MAINS (primary) fuse** — the design's other fuse is only on the secondary. Required for safety.
- ⚠️ **Chassis fit:** the board is **190×115 mm** (matches the original 25-5274-2). At ~53 % packing it's dense but feasible there; it does **not** fit the Part 7 155×90 "safe-bet" (~90 %). Measure the real chassis, or move passives to SMD, before committing to a size.

## Key constraints
- No new holes in original panels; all improvements internal only.
- MRB hardwired to 600 Hz (original spec); tremolo rate LED is PCB-mounted (diagnostic).
- Original 6-pin DIN footswitch connector and pedal retained.
