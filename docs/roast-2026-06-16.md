# Roast pass — 2026-06-16

An adversarial review. Assume nothing the prior passes claimed is right until the
math or the file says so. Each finding below is **verified** (calculation or file
reference), with severity and a fix. Includes errors the *previous audit (mine)
introduced* — those get the harshest light.

Legend: 🔴 must-fix (safety / spec-breaking) · 🟠 real design defect · 🟡 hygiene/clarity.

## 🔴 R1 — No mains (primary) fuse. Safety gap.
The only fuse in the whole design is **F1, 1 A slow-blow, on the secondary DC rail**
(`VRAW → +33V5`, i.e. *after* the bridge). There is **no primary/mains fuse**.
A mains-powered amplifier must fuse the primary for fire protection (IEC 60065 /
UL); a secondary-rail fuse does not protect against a shorted transformer primary,
a failed bridge, or a mains fault.
- **Fix (done):** add a **mains primary fuse** in the IEC inlet / before the
  transformer primary. For the ~50 VA transformer: **T630 mA–T1 A, 250 V (5×20 mm)
  slow-blow at 120 V** (≈ T400 mA at 230 V). Added to the BOM and the Part 1
  safety + Part 3 wiring docs. It is panel/inlet-mounted (off-board), like the
  transformer.

## 🔴 R2 — "18 W into 8 Ω" is physically unreachable on a 33.5 V single supply.
Verified: 18 W into 8 Ω needs `Vpeak = √(2·18·8) = 17.0 V`, but a single 33.5 V
rail only offers **16.75 V** peak swing to the rail *at zero saturation*. With the
LM1875's real ~2–4 V saturation the ceiling is:

| Vsat | Vpeak | Pmax (8 Ω) |
|----:|----:|----:|
| 0 V | 16.75 V | 17.5 W (absolute) |
| 2 V | 14.75 V | 13.6 W |
| 3 V | 13.75 V | 11.8 W |
| 4 V | 12.75 V | 10.2 W |

So the rebuild makes **~12 W clean (1 % THD), ~14 W at clipping — never 18 W.**
Perceptually that's only ~1.8 dB down, but the number is propagated as fact into
the engineering:
- **Part 5 thermal** computes LM1875 dissipation "at 18 W into 8 Ω" — an operating
  point that can't occur (conservative, so the heatsink is over-spec'd = safe, but
  the premise is wrong).
- **`component-availability-audit.md` §3 (my error, last pass):** stated "at 18 W,
  peak output current 2.1 A" and "~18 W out ⇒ ~50 VA transformer." Both built on the
  unreachable 18 W. Real numbers: ~12 W out ⇒ peak ~1.7 A, ~0.5 A average ⇒ ~20 W
  draw ⇒ **~30 VA minimum** (the 50 VA AnTek is then comfortable headroom, not a
  tight match). LM1875 worst-case dissipation is *lower* than computed, so the
  ≤2.5 °C/W heatsink is over-spec'd (fine).
- **Fix (done):** corrected Part 5, Part 1 §3, and the availability audit; the
  original-amp spec table keeps 18 W as the *germanium amp's* (10 %-THD) figure
  with a note that the LM1875 rebuild ceilings at ~12 W. If the full 18 W is a
  hard requirement, it needs a different topology (bridged LM1875, or LM3886 /
  TDA7293 on a higher rail) — out of scope, flagged.

## 🟠 R3 — The reverb dry/wet mixer is broken as drawn.
Reverb blend (reverb sheet): `DRY = TONE_OUT —R_dry_tap(1M)→ —R_blend1(220k)→ BLEND`
and `WET —R_blend2(220k)→ BLEND`. The **1 MΩ series resistor in the dry path**
attenuates dry ~5× harder than wet (220 k) → the mix is effectively **all-wet**.
Worse, `BLEND` is an unbuffered high-Z node (~110 kΩ) driving straight into the
tremolo `R_trem1 (10 k)` + LDR → massive signal loss and a hum-prone node. This is
the concrete face of "**ERC-clean ≠ designed**": the inter-effect chain was wired
for connectivity, not for levels/impedance.
- **✅ RESOLVED (2026-06-16):** rebuilt as a real **active inverting summer** on the
  spare **IC1-B** half — dry always on, wet level via `POT_REV`, unity each
  (R_drymix = R_wetmix = R_mixfb = 100 k), output low-Z about `VBIAS_R`. The old
  passive `R_dry_tap`/`R_blend1`/`R_blend2` are gone. A second spare half, **IC2-B**,
  now buffers the post-MRB output into the power amp (was the unbuffered `R_painput`
  1 M). **Verified with a rail-aware model** (`spice/tran_reverb_mixer.cir`,
  `models/opamp_rail.sub`): output biases at **8.50 V** (mid-rail), **0.0 dB** unity
  sum, and clips cleanly at **15.5 V / 1.5 V** → **±7 V headroom** (~24 dB over a
  0.42 V program peak). ERC 0.

## 🟠 R4 — `Wiring_Edge_Array_35` footprint is orphaned.
Defined in `footprints/cambridge_reverb.pretty/`, advertised in `kicad/README.md`
and Part 10 as a deliverable — but **never placed on any board** (`gen_pcb.py`
uses individual pin-header connectors for the off-board wiring). Dead artifact /
overstated deliverable.
- **Fix (done):** README now marks it **unused/reference** (kept for a future
  single-edge wiring layout); it is no longer listed as if it's on the board.

## 🟡 R5 — F1 sits in the C_main inrush path.
F1 (1 A SB) is between the bridge and `C_main` (4700 µF), so it eats the full
turn-on charging surge of 4700 µF on every power-up → nuisance-blow risk and slow
degradation. **Recommend** moving the secondary fuse to the **AC secondary**
(before the bridge), and sizing for inrush. Documented; not auto-changed (it's a
topology choice). Note this is the *secondary* fuse — it does not substitute for
the mains fuse (R1).

## 🟡 R6 — Shared `VBIAS` can bleed tremolo into reverb.
One `R_vb1/R_vb2` (100 k/100 k) divider, bypassed by `C_vb` 10 µF, feeds **both**
IC1 (reverb) and IC2 (tremolo LFO) non-inverting refs. C_vb + 50 kΩ source = a
**0.3 Hz** corner, but the LFO band (1–16 Hz) is *above* it, so LFO current draw
modulates `VBIAS` → the reverb stage rides on a wobbling reference → audible
tremolo bleed into reverb when the LFO runs.
- **✅ RESOLVED (2026-06-16):** split into **two independent dividers** — `VBIAS_R`
  (reverb IC1) and `VBIAS_T` (tremolo IC2), each 100 k/100 k + **47 µF** bypass. The
  LFO now draws from `VBIAS_T` only; it cannot reach the reverb reference at all
  (different nets). ERC 0.

## 🟡 R7 — "Tool-verified" oversells the SPICE.
`models/opamp1p.sub` is a **rail-less** one-pole macromodel. So every single-supply
behaviour that actually matters — output headroom, clipping onset, whether the
reverb driver can swing enough, the real power-amp ceiling (R2!) — is **not
simulated**. "Verified with SPICE" means: small-signal gain/bandwidth of *isolated*
blocks with idealized models. The integrated chain and all large-signal behaviour
are unverified. The SPICE README now says this plainly.

## 🟡 R8 — CHANGELOG is incoherent.
A floating `## Audit pass (2026-06-14)` section sits above `## Unreleased`, which
itself holds dated `### …` subsections — **all stamped 2026-06-14** though the work
spans 06-14 → 06-16. Confusing order, wrong dates. **Fix (done):** consolidated
under `## Unreleased` in chronological order with correct dates.

---
## Scorecard
The project remains an excellent *documentation + tooling* artifact and an
ERC-clean schematic / placed board. But this pass shows the **electrical design is
not build-ready**: a safety-critical fuse is missing (R1), the headline power spec
is unachievable (R2), and the effect-chain mix is non-functional as drawn (R3).
"Reconstructed, verify before building" (PROVENANCE.md) is doing a lot of load-
bearing work — treat the signal chain and power section as *design intent*, not a
validated circuit.
